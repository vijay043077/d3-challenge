Correlations Discovered Between Poverty and Obesity %

We can observe that our data for obesity rates is gradually effected by poverty rates in this study.

Obesity in this study hovers around 30% regardless of income/ poverty factors as observed in our largest cluster. Poverty rates in this cluster hover around the 15% mark. People with the highest levels of income (least poverty) can be among the highest in rate of obesity, however, those are mostly outliers. Generally the data shows a gradual increase in rate of obesity among higher poverty rates.

On the low end of the scale, a poverty rate of 12% and obesity rate of 21% is observed, on the high end of the scale, a poverty rate of 20% and obesity rate of 36% is observed. That information coupled with the observed largest clustered range (above) provide a good picture of the relationships between these two factors.
